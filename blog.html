From Swamp to City

Date: August 5, 2025

Nairobi began as a humble railway depot in 1899 during the construction of the Uganda Railway. Its strategic location between Mombasa and Uganda led to rapid growth, and by 1905 it had replaced Mombasa as the capital of British East Africa. The name "Nairobi" comes from the Maasai phrase "Enkare Nairobi," meaning "cool water," in reference to the Nairobi River.

Colonial Legacy and Growth

Date: August 3, 2025

Under colonial rule, Nairobi expanded as a commercial and administrative center. However, its growth also mirrored racial segregation and socio-economic divides. After independence in 1963, the city began to evolve into a modern African metropolis, blending colonial architecture with contemporary development.

Modern Nairobi

Date: August 1, 2025

Today, Nairobi is a hub of finance, technology, and culture in East Africa. It's home to thousands of startups, the UN's Africa headquarters, and a diverse population. Despite challenges like traffic congestion and urban sprawl, Nairobi continues to reinvent itself with smart city projects and green spaces like the Nairobi National Park.
